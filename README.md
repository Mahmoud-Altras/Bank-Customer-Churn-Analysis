Executive Summary: Bank Customer Churn Analysis

Phase 1: Data Integrity \& Baseline Metrics

* Data Quality: Verified 10,127 records. Conducted rigorous integrity checks yielding 0 duplicates (Primary Key validation) and 0 NULL values in the target variable.
* Current State: The global customer attrition rate stands at 16.07%.
* Product Distribution: The overwhelming majority of the customer base relies on the entry-level 'Blue' credit card.

Phase 2: Demographic Risk Profiling

* Gender Variance: Female churn rate (17.35%) outpaces the male churn rate (14.61%). Note: Analysis relies strictly on relative percentages, mitigating the bias of absolute volume comparisons.
* Income Correlation: Contrary to standard assumptions, the highest-income tier (+$120K) exhibits the highest attrition rate at 17.33%.

Phase 3: Behavioral Indicators (The Red Flags)

* Usage Velocity: Churn is a gradual withdrawal, not a sudden event. Attrited customers show a severe drop in average annual transactions (declining from 69 to 45).
* Quarterly Collapse: High-risk customers display a \~45% collapse in transaction volume between Q1 and Q4.
* Missed Up-selling: 95.2% of the female customer base remains on the basic Blue card, indicating a critical failure in matching product tier to client wealth.

Phase 4: Actionable Recommendations

1. Automated Early Warning System: Deploy an automated trigger to send gamified promotions (e.g., cashback, zero-interest installments) exactly when a high-income customer hits the 30-transaction mark.
2. VIP Outbound Retention: Eliminate passive email surveys for the +$120K segment. Deploy top-tier outbound agents for proactive "Concierge Check-in" calls to resolve friction points before the account is closed.
3. Product Repositioning: Initiate an auto-upgrade campaign, granting Gold or Platinum status free for the first year to high-income Blue cardholders to increase switching costs and loyalty.
